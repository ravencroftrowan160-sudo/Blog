Authoritative parenting

Authoritative parenting is a parenting style characterized by high responsiveness and high demands. Parents who use this style set clear standards and limits for their children while also being supportive and nurturing. This approach promotes independence, self-regulation, and social competence in children.

Conscientiousness

Conscientiousness is a personality trait characterized by being organized, responsible, and dependable. Individuals high in conscientiousness tend to be diligent, disciplined, and goal-oriented, often exhibiting a strong work ethic and attention to detail.

Displacement

Displacement is a defense mechanism in which an individual redirects their emotions or impulses from a threatening or unacceptable target to a safer or more acceptable one. For example, a person who is angry at their boss may take out their frustration on a family member instead.

Teratogens

Teratogens are agents or factors that cause malformation of an embryo. They can be substances such as drugs, chemicals, or infections that a pregnant woman is exposed to during the critical period of fetal development.

Secure attachment

Secure attachment is a strong, healthy emotional bond between a child and their caregiver. It is characterized by the child's confidence in the caregiver's availability and responsiveness, leading to feelings of safety and security.

Self-concept

Self-concept refers to the overall idea or perception that an individual has about themselves. It encompasses beliefs, feelings, and thoughts about one's own identity, abilities, and worth.
